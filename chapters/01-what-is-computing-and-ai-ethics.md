## Section 1: What makes a computing decision an ethical decision?

::: {.section-question}
Question: When does a technical choice become a moral choice?
:::

Technical decisions become ethical decisions when they influence who benefits, who is burdened, and which values are prioritized by default. In practice, this means choices about training data, optimization goals, interface design, and deployment context are never value-neutral. Contemporary scholarship shows broad international agreement on principles such as fairness, accountability, and transparency, while also revealing real disagreement about how to implement those principles in institutions and law [@jobin2019global]. Professional standards similarly frame computing as a public-interest activity rather than purely a technical exercise [@acm2018code].

## Case Study

::: {.case-study}
**Case:** Recommendation algorithm in a school platform

- Context: A school system deploys AI tools to recommend learning pathways.
- Stakeholders: Students, teachers, families, administrators, vendors.
- Ethical tension: Efficiency and personalization vs fairness and student autonomy.
- Questions for analysis: Which outcomes count as harm, and who decides?
:::
