# Policy on AI-Assisted Writing

This project permits AI-assisted drafting and editing, with transparency and verification requirements.

## Core Principles

1. **Transparency:** Substantive AI assistance should be disclosed in commit messages or chapter notes.
2. **Verification:** Human authors must verify factual claims, citations, and interpretations before publication.
3. **Accountability:** Named human maintainers remain responsible for all final content.
4. **Attribution:** AI systems are tools, not authors; authorship credit belongs to human contributors.
5. **Source integrity:** Do not cite nonexistent sources. Every citation key must resolve to a real entry in `refs/references.bib`.

## Required Practices

- Check every AI-generated citation for bibliographic correctness.
- Remove unverified claims and placeholders before release builds.
- Keep normative arguments attributable to specific authors, texts, or clearly labeled original analysis.
- Document major AI-assisted revisions in commit history.

## Prohibited Practices

- Fabricating quotations, page numbers, or bibliographic entries.
- Presenting AI output as peer-reviewed scholarship.
- Using copyrighted images without permission or a valid open license.
